About

Hey! So you're curious to know more about me. Read on, and I'll tell you.

I'm currently living in the Chicago-area metro - and offering my services as a software consultant, interface designer, and event planner - but mostly, I'm looking to provide the first two.

I've been working on the web professionally since 2004, when I launched Bobby Horton.com. I've continued to build, deploy, and optimize web applications for many clients since then. I'm well versed in Ruby (and Rails), PHP, Python, and Javascript. Most recently, I worked with Mad Mimi, an email marketing startup. In a short five month period, we were able to dramatically increase performance, and increase the number of users - on both the site and API.

In 2005, I launched a podcast that quickly rose to popularity thanks to the iTunes Podcast directory. Though that show is no longer on the air, I retained my love for podcasting - going on to launch The Machine, a show featuring the best and brightest independent music from artists around the world. (The podcast website is being reconstructed at the moment.) I'm also currently offering my voice-over services as the co-host of the popular Mothpod podcast.

After moving to Chicago in June, 2010 - I started laying plans for the Chicago Geek Breakfast - a monthly gathering of photographers, software developers, designers, and researchers. Now in it's fourth month, the group grows with every meeting.

When I'm not writing code, planning events, recording a podcast, or consulting with clients - you'll find me enjoying craft beer or artisan spirits, reading, or exploring the great city where I live.

Quick Hits

A few questions designed to help you get acquainted with who I am.

What is your web development framework of choice?

It depends on what I'm building. For most projects, I find Ruby on Rails works the best. It's eloquent, well-established, and allows me to quickly deliver working software.

That said, both developers and consumers are demanding more from their online experience - and I see myself using platforms like Node.js (a light and fast way to write server-side applications in javascript) more in the future. (I'm currently working with Node on one project, and absolutely loving it.)

What's your preferred editor?

Ah, another geek in the crowd. Well, from one geek to another - I love VIM. No - before you ask, I don't want to discuss eMacs.) Wanna see my current VIM configuration?

How important is validation, when writing HTML?

While important, validation isn't the gold standard for evaluating anyone's markup. Don't get me wrong, every site I write validates in pure HTML5. However, we're still in an environment where certain "hacks" are required to support certain (often legacy) browsers. These workarounds may prevent markup from validating properly - but the markup may render as desired in all browsers despite failing validation.

The biggest question is - how important is support for legacy browsers? This is a question I'll ask in the beginning, and we'll move forward accordingly.
